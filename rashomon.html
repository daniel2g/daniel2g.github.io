Rashōmon
This poster for the 1950 film “Rashōmon” is a striking visual metaphor for the walls we build to protect ourselves from suffering. I use manipulated bricks to convey the artifice inherent in the rememory of trauma. As four characters become entangled in a violent crime, it becomes clear there is no single story of the course of events, with each interpretation built on a different web of myopic projections.
