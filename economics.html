Ecological Economics
This typeset and illustrated edition of Daly and Farley’s textbook proposes a forward-thinking view of economics. Although originally published in 2004, the book’s message is more relevant than ever, breaking from the status quo of econ textbooks and representing a necessary change to include the social and environmental implications of economics.
The book demanded a contemporary design that reflects the urgency of the content. I created a simple and repeatable visual language using ellipses to represent the economy as existing within the bounds of the earth (or conversely, the earth’s resources existing within the ever-expanding economy). The concepts this motif elucidates are central to understanding how this text differs from mainstream texts.
Since this book gives readers the conceptual framework to discuss broader economic issues, the hand-drawn look of the diagrams and custom lettering implies that each of us has the power to rewrite the story of economics.
