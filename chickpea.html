The Chickpea
Three illustrated spreads of a poem by Rumi. In Islamic and Jewish art alike, the prohibition against images inspired pattern designs which reflect the infinity of the universe without making representations of divine figures. To present this poem, which uses food as metaphor, I created patterns of kitchen utensils rendered in rich earth tones. By replacing traditional geometric or vegetal motifs with more accessible imagery, I leave the reader curious and connected to Rumi’s words.
