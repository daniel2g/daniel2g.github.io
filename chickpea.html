The Chickpea
Three illustrated spreads of a poem by Rumi. In the history of Islamic art, the prohibition against images inspired pattern designs which reflect the infinity of the universe without making representations of divine figures. To present this poem, which uses food as metaphor, I created patterns of kitchen utensils rendered in rich earth tones. By replacing traditional geometric or vegetal motifs with more modern imagery, I aim to leave the reader curious and connected to Rumi’s words.
