Calligraphc Blur
I designed this typeface under the guidance of Richard Lipton at RISD. Beginning my research by cutting shapes out of paper, I noticed the speed of my motion softening corners and expanding negative space like points of light through a defocused lens. I became interested in the ways processes translate and distort letterforms like a game of ‘telephone’ and looked at plastic alphabet refrigerator magnets as an example of roundness as a by-product of production. Using the imagined ductus of a broad-edged pen as the underlying structure of my glyphs, I blurred the contours by drawing inflated serifs and eroded connections. The challenge was in ensuring the blobs of weight coalesce into clear, readable text.
