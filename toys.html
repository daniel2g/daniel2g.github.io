Toys, Change, and the Business of Culture
This playful speculative book cover and interior layout places Shindana dolls on a shag rug, emphasizing the ordinary domestic context in which toys’ cultural narratives often go unquestioned. I connected, as the book does, the expansion of American visual culture during the Civil Rights and Anti-War movements to the everyday commerce in which we partake.
